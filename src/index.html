Utah Hackers

A collaborative community excited by learning new technologies and increasing our breadth and depth of experience in all aspects of software development.

About Utah Hackers

One of the best ways to learn anything is to do it. Utah Hackers facilitates members working together on prototypes, toy projects, big ideas, and generally any software project they can share with the group. Projects don't have to be officially open source, but they should not be anything related to an NDA, or anything people would fight over if it started making money.

Meeting Formats

Meetings follow one of two formats:

Second Tuesday of every Month: Updates and Pitches

Updates:

Any attendee can take the floor for up to 15 minutes to give an update or demo of the project they've been working on.

Pitches:

Any attendee can take the floor and explain:

what they're working on, and would like help with
-OR-
something they know, or would like to learn, and see if anyone has a related project they could help with
-OR-
a topic they want to have a discussion about

Open Hack:

Grab someone who's pitch you liked and get some work done, talk to one of the presenters about their project, whatever...

All Other Tuesdays: Open Hack

Meet your collaborators in our space and get some work done. Use Utah Hackers as an accountability group to make sure you make progress on your project every week.

Meetings are open to:

  • technologists of all experience levels
  • creators using any technology
  • only nice people, no jerks
